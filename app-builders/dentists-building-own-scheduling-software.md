# TIL: Domain experts are now building their own niche software with Claude — and that changes the competitive bar

**Category:** app-builders

---

## What I learned

A dentist, frustrated that scheduling platforms treat his day as a flat calendar (when he's actually hands-on for only 50–60% of any procedure, with hygienist time and insurance paperwork in between), fed Claude his exact workflow, constraints, and procedure-specific timing variables. Over one long weekend he had a working prototype tuned to his practice's rhythm — which then became more valuable as a *training tool* for his receptionists than as software itself, because it let them finally see why the schedule should be structured a certain way.

## Why it matters

Software is merging with non-technical fields from the non-technical side: people in clinics, law firms, logistics, and farms can now build working tools for problems their vendors never solved (the market was too fragmented, variables too clinic-specific). "Build it once and ride it" is over — incumbents (e.g. Eagle Soft in dental scheduling) that stopped solving the underlying workflow problem are exactly what creates the opening. Customers now compare your product to the personalized workflow they could build themselves, and some will. The hard part shifts from building to noticing what your customers have already started building without you.
